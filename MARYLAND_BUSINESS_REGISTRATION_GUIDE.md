## PART 1: THE DOPE CLOUD TEACHER (For-Profit LLC)

### Step 1: Choose Business Structure
**Recommendation: LLC (Limited Liability Company)**
- Personal asset protection
- Pass-through taxation (profits on personal return)
- Less paperwork than Corporation
- Professional image

### Step 2: Maryland LLC Formation (Cost: ~$100-150)

#### A. Choose Your Business Name
**Name:** The Dope Cloud Teacher, LLC

**Check Availability:**
1. Visit: https://egov.maryland.gov/BusinessExpress/EntitySearch
2. Search "The Dope Cloud Teacher"
3. If available, reserve it ($25 for 30 days - optional)

**Alternative Names (if taken):**
- Dope Cloud Teacher, LLC
- The Dope Cloud Academy, LLC
- Dope Cloud Education, LLC

#### B. File Articles of Organization
**Online Filing (Recommended):**
1. Go to: https://egov.maryland.gov/businessexpress/
2. Click "Start a New Business"
3. Select "Limited Liability Company (LLC)"
4. Fill out form:
   - **LLC Name:** The Dope Cloud Teacher, LLC
   - **Principal Office Address:** Your home address (can use virtual office)
   - **Resident Agent:** Yourself OR hire registered agent service ($100-300/year)
   - **Purpose:** Educational services, online training, course development
   - **Management:** Member-managed (you run it)
   
**Filing Fee:** $100 (online)
**Processing Time:** 7-10 business days (expedited available for extra $100)

#### C. Get Your EIN (Free!)
**You mentioned you started this - let's complete it:**

1. Go to: https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online
2. Click "Apply Online Now"
3. Select "Limited Liability Company"
4. Answer questions:
   - Business name: The Dope Cloud Teacher, LLC
   - Reason: Started new business
   - Owner: Rosee Murphy (your name)
   - Number of members: 1 (if just you)
5. **Get EIN immediately** (save the letter!)

**Why You Need EIN:**
- Open business bank account
- Apply for business credit
- File taxes
- Accept payments via Gumroad/Stripe
- Apply for grants

### Step 3: Maryland State Tax Registration

#### A. Combined Registration Application
**Website:** https://interactive.marylandtaxes.gov/
1. Register for Maryland Central Registration number
2. Select taxes you need:
   - Sales & Use Tax (if selling digital products in MD)
   - Withholding Tax (if hiring employees)

**Timeline:** Immediate online

#### B. Maryland Department of Labor Registration
If hiring employees (even yourself):
1. Visit: https://www.dllr.state.md.us/
2. Register for unemployment insurance
3. Get account number

### Step 4: Business Licenses & Permits

**Good News:** Online education businesses in Maryland typically don't need special licenses!

**Check if you need:**
- Home Occupation Permit (if working from home - check county)
- Business License (some Maryland counties require)

**Your County:** Check with your local county clerk:
- Baltimore County: https://www.baltimorecountymd.gov/
- Montgomery County: https://www.montgomerycountymd.gov/
- Prince George's County: https://www.princegeorgescountymd.gov/

**Cost:** $0-100 depending on county

### Step 5: Open Business Bank Account

**Required Documents:**
- EIN letter from IRS
- Maryland Articles of Organization (with filing stamp)
- LLC Operating Agreement (create this - template below)
- Your driver's license

**Recommended Banks:**
- **Chase Business Complete Banking** - $15/month or free with $2K balance
- **Bank of America Business Advantage** - Good for business credit building
- **Capital One Spark Business** - Good for online businesses
- **Mercury** (online) - Popular with tech startups, $0/month

**What to bring:**
- $100-500 opening deposit
- All documents above

### Step 6: Get Business Insurance

**General Liability Insurance:**
- Protects against lawsuits
- **Cost:** $300-600/year
- **Providers:** Hiscox, Next Insurance, FLIP Insurance

**Professional Liability (E&O):**
- Protects against claims of bad training/advice
- **Cost:** $500-1,000/year

**Get Quote:** https://www.hiscox.com/ or https://www.nextinsurance.com/

## PART 2: ASKDOGOOD.COM (501c3 Nonprofit)

### Step 1: Define Your Mission

**Example Mission Statement:**
"askdogood empowers underserved communities through free technology education, bridging the digital divide and creating pathways to tech careers."

**Key Questions:**
1. Who do you serve? (Low-income, minorities, rural communities, etc.)
2. What problem do you solve? (Lack of tech training, career access, etc.)
3. How do you solve it? (Free courses, mentorship, job placement)

### Step 2: Form Maryland Nonprofit Corporation

#### A. File Articles of Incorporation (Nonprofit)
**Website:** https://egov.maryland.gov/businessexpress/
1. Select "Nonstock Corporation" (nonprofit structure)
2. Fill out form:
   - **Name:** askdogood, Inc. or Ask Do Good Foundation
   - **Purpose:** Charitable, educational (be specific about mission)
   - **Initial Directors:** Need at least 3 (you + 2 board members)
   - **Resident Agent:** Yourself or service

**Filing Fee:** $100-120
**Timeline:** 7-10 business days

#### B. Create Required Documents

**1. Articles of Incorporation** (filed with state)
Must include:
- 501(c)(3) language (specific IRS requirements)
- Dissolution clause (assets go to another nonprofit)
- No private benefit clause

**2. Bylaws** (internal document)
- Board structure (minimum 3 directors)
- Meeting requirements
- Voting procedures
- Officer roles
- Conflict of interest policy

**3. Conflict of Interest Policy**
Required by IRS for 501(c)(3)

**Templates:** https://www.irs.gov/charities-non-profits/sample-conflict-of-interest-policy

### Step 3: Get EIN for Nonprofit

Same process as LLC, but select:
- Entity type: "Nonprofit Organization"
- Reason: "Other" ‚Üí "Applying for 501(c)(3) status"

### Step 4: Apply for 501(c)(3) Tax-Exempt Status

**This is THE BIG ONE for grants!**

#### Option A: Form 1023-EZ (Simplified)
**Eligibility:**
- Projected annual gross receipts < $50,000 for first 3 years
- Assets < $250,000

**Cost:** $275 filing fee
**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks approval
**Apply:** https://www.irs.gov/forms-pubs/about-form-1023-ez

#### Option B: Form 1023 (Full Application)
**When to use:**
- Larger budget
- Complex operations
- Want to be taken more seriously by funders

**Cost:** $600 filing fee
**Timeline:** 3-6 months (sometimes longer)
**Help:** Consider hiring nonprofit attorney ($2,000-5,000) or use:
- **DIY:** https://www.irs.gov/forms-pubs/about-form-1023
- **Service:** Harbor Compliance ($1,500 + filing fee)

**My Recommendation for You:** Start with 1023-EZ to get approved fast, then build up operations.

### Step 5: Maryland Charitable Organization Registration

**REQUIRED in Maryland to solicit donations!**

1. Visit: https://www.sos.state.md.us/Charity/CharityRegistration.aspx
2. Register with Secretary of State - Charitable Organizations Division
3. File **Initial Registration** - Form COI
4. **Cost:** $0 (free!)
5. **Annual Renewal:** Required

### Step 6: Open Nonprofit Bank Account

**Documents Needed:**
- EIN letter
- Articles of Incorporation
- Bylaws
- IRS 501(c)(3) determination letter (when received)
- List of board members

**Bank Recommendations:**
- Chase Business Complete (free for nonprofits)
- Bank of America (nonprofit programs)
- Local credit unions (often best for nonprofits)

## PART 4: BUILDING BUSINESS CREDIT (ALL BUSINESSES)

### Why Build Business Credit?
- Access to business loans
- Higher credit limits
- Better terms with vendors
- Separate from personal credit

### Step 1: Dun & Bradstreet (D&B) Registration

**What:** D&B assigns DUNS number (like business credit score)

**How to Register:**
1. Go to: https://www.dnb.com/duns-number/get-a-duns.html
2. Click "Get Your Free DUNS Number"
3. Provide:
   - Business legal name
   - Physical address (not PO Box)
   - Phone number
   - EIN
4. **Free** for basic registration
5. Receive DUNS number in 30 days

**Paid Option:** D&B CreditBuilder ($149/month)
- Monitor your business credit
- Update business information
- Faster DUNS number

### Step 2: NAV Business Credit Monitoring

**What:** Free business credit monitoring and building tools

**Setup:**
1. Go to: https://www.nav.com/
2. Sign up (free account)
3. Enter business information
4. Link bank account (verify business activity)
5. Get free credit score monitoring from:
   - Dun & Bradstreet
   - Experian Business
   - Equifax Business

**Premium:** $29-99/month for advanced features

### Step 3: Experian Business Registration

1. Visit: https://www.experian.com/business-information/business-credit-reports.html
2. Claim your business profile
3. Add business information
4. Monitor credit reports

### Step 4: Build Business Credit (6-Month Plan)

**Month 1-2:**
- Get business phone line (not personal) - $15-30/month
  - Grasshopper, Google Voice Business, RingCentral
- Get business address (if using home, consider virtual office)
  - Regus, Opus Virtual Offices ($50-150/month)
- Set up business website (you have this!)
- Get DUNS number

**Month 3-4:**
- Open vendor accounts that report to credit bureaus:
  - **Uline** (office supplies) - Reports to D&B
  - **Quill** (office supplies) - Reports to D&B, Experian
  - **Grainger** (industrial supplies) - Reports to D&B
- Make small purchases, pay on time

**Month 5-6:**
- Apply for business credit card (starter):
  - **Capital One Spark Classic** (easiest approval)
  - **Brex** (no personal guarantee)
  - **American Express Blue Business Cash**
- Use for business expenses, pay in full monthly

**Month 7-12:**
- Build payment history
- Apply for higher-limit cards
- Get business line of credit
