## PART 4: GRANT WRITING STRATEGY

### Before You Apply - Foundation Work

**1. Get Organized (Week 1)**
- [ ] Create grant tracking spreadsheet
- [ ] Set up grant calendar with deadlines
- [ ] Gather all business documents
- [ ] Write your "case for support" (why you deserve funding)

**2. Build Your Grant-Ready Toolkit**

**Essential Documents (prepare these ONCE, reuse everywhere):**
- [ ] **Executive Summary** (1 page) - Business overview
- [ ] **Problem Statement** (2 pages) - What problem you solve
- [ ] **Program Description** (3-5 pages) - What you do
- [ ] **Budget Narrative** (1-2 pages) - How you'll spend money
- [ ] **Evaluation Plan** (1-2 pages) - How you'll measure success
- [ ] **Board of Directors list** (for nonprofit)
- [ ] **Letters of Support** (3-5 from community partners)
- [ ] **Financial Statements** (or projections if new)
- [ ] **IRS Determination Letter** (501c3 proof)
- [ ] **Annual Report** (create simple one)

**Templates:** I'll create these for you below!

### The Grant Application Process

**Step 1: Research (Time: 2-3 hours per grant)**
- Read ENTIRE grant guidelines
- Check eligibility requirements
- Review past grant recipients
- Confirm deadline and format

**Step 2: Letter of Inquiry (LOI) - If Required**
- 2-3 pages
- Problem, solution, request, impact
- Decision in 4-8 weeks

**Step 3: Full Proposal (if invited)**
- 5-15 pages typically
- Follow guidelines EXACTLY
- Answer every question
- Include all required attachments

**Step 4: Budget**
- Detailed line-item budget
- Match funder's format
- Be realistic!
- Show sustainability plan

**Step 5: Submit & Follow Up**
- Submit early (avoid last-minute tech issues)
- Send thank you note
- Follow up in 2-3 weeks
- Be patient (decisions take 2-6 months)

### Grant Writing Tips for Success

**‚úÖ DO:**
- Start with funder's priorities, not yours
- Use data and statistics
- Tell compelling stories (real people impacted)
- Be specific about outcomes (# people served, % improvement)
- Proofread MULTIPLE times
- Follow page limits and formatting rules
- Submit early

**‚ùå DON'T:**
- Use jargon or acronyms
- Make it all about you (focus on community impact)
- Ignore the guidelines
- Submit incomplete applications
- Apply to grants you're not eligible for
- Copy-paste without customizing

## PART 7: GRANT APPLICATION TEMPLATES

### Template 1: Executive Summary (1 Page)

```
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Organization: [askdogood / The Dope Cloud Teacher]
Request Amount: $[amount]
Project Duration: [timeframe]

THE PROBLEM:
[3-4 sentences about the problem you're solving]
Example: "Only 3% of African Americans and 2% of Hispanics work in cloud computing roles, despite these being among the fastest-growing, highest-paying careers. Traditional barriers include lack of access to training, high certification costs, and limited mentorship opportunities."

OUR SOLUTION:
[3-4 sentences about your program]
Example: "The Dope Cloud Teacher provides free, comprehensive cloud computing education to underserved communities in Maryland. Our program includes hands-on training, certification preparation, mentorship, and job placement assistance."

PROJECT GOALS:
- Train [X] individuals in cloud computing skills
- Achieve [X]% certification pass rate
- Place [X]% of graduates in jobs earning $60K+
- Expand to [X] additional communities

BUDGET REQUEST:
$[amount] will fund:
- [Item 1]: $X
- [Item 2]: $X
- [Item 3]: $X

IMPACT:
This grant will enable us to [specific impact]. Without this funding, [what won't happen].

SUSTAINABILITY:
After grant period, we will sustain through [revenue streams, additional grants, partnerships].
```
